🏗️ 1. Hormonal Foundation

The bedrock. Without optimized hormones, everything else underperforms.

Testosterone Replacement Therapy (TRT) ⭐ S-Tier
Dosage
100–200mg/week (trough 700–1000 ng/dL)
Timing
Split 2–3x/week (Mon/Wed/Fri)
Route
IM or SubQ (25–29g needle)
Cycle
Continuous (lifelong on TRT)

Mechanism: Binds androgen receptors — drives protein synthesis, nitrogen retention, RBC production, libido, mood, cognitive function. Enhanced fat loss via increased lipolysis.

Synergies: HCG (testicular function), Boron (reduces SHBG → more free T), Zinc (5α-reductase support), Creatine (DHT + performance).

Side Effects: Polycythemia (donate blood if hematocrit >52%), acne, hair thinning, testicular atrophy (mitigate with HCG), estrogen elevation.

Bloodwork: Total T, Free T, SHBG, Estradiol (sensitive), Hematocrit, PSA, Lipids.

Human Chorionic Gonadotropin (HCG) ⭐ S-Tier
Dosage
250–500 IU, 2–3x/week
Timing
On TRT injection days or alternating
Route
SubQ (abdominal)
Cycle
Continuous alongside TRT
Source
Peptide Sciences / Rx clinic

Mechanism: Mimics LH → stimulates Leydig cells → preserves intratesticular T + pregnenolone + DHEA production + testicular volume + fertility.

Why essential: TRT-only → testes atrophy, neurosteroid production drops. Many men feel "flat" despite good T numbers. HCG fills this gap. Gold standard adjunct per AUA guidelines.

Side Effects: Estrogen elevation (HCG-derived T aromatizes heavily), water retention, nipple sensitivity. May need AI adjustment.
