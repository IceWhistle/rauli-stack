## 3. SENOLYTIC PROTOCOLS

### Dasatinib + Quercetin (DQ)

#### Clinical Trial Protocols
| Protocol | Dose | Schedule | Duration |
|----------|------|----------|----------|
| Mayo Clinic | D: 100mg + Q: 1250mg | 2 days on, 5 days off | 3 weeks |
| Intermittent | D: 100mg + Q: 500mg | 3 days on, 25 days off | Monthly |

#### Current Research Findings (2024)
- DQ treatment showed **increased epigenetic age acceleration** in first-gen clocks at 3-6 months
- **Telomere length decreased** during treatment
- Adding **Fisetin** mitigated the epigenetic age effects
- Improved pulmonary function, physical performance

### Fisetin Protocol

#### Standard Dosing
- **High-dose pulse:** 100-150 mg/kg for 2-3 consecutive days
- **Maintenance:** 500-1000 mg daily
- **Monthly protocol:** 100-150 mg/kg for 2-3 days, then 25+ days off

#### Life Extension Protocol
- **Option 1:** 100 mg Dasatinib for 3 days + 1000 mg Quercetin for 3 days
- **Option 2:** 1500 mg Fisetin for 3 days
- **Max protocol:** All three compounds on rotating schedule

#### Evidence Level: MODERATE-HIGH
- Fisetin shown to be **safest and most potent natural senolytic** (Mayo Clinic, 2018)
- Extends median and maximum lifespan in mice
- Reduces senescence markers in human tissues

## KEY TAKEAWAYS

### What Actually Works (Evidence-Based)
1. **Lifestyle interventions** have strongest evidence
2. **Ca-AKG** shows real epigenetic age reversal
3. **Senolytics** remove aged cells but epigenetic effects complex
4. **Rapamycin** promising but requires careful monitoring
5. **Urolithin A** emerging as mitochondrial game-changer

### What to Avoid
1. Continuous high-dose rapamycin (immune suppression)
2. Daily senolytics (toxicity concerns)
3. Unmonitored hormone therapies
4. Synthetic vs. natural spermidine (quality matters)

### Monitoring Recommended
- Regular biological age testing (Horvath, DunedinPACE)
- Inflammation markers (hsCRP)
- Metabolic panel (glucose, insulin, lipids)
- Hormone panels
- Regular blood work every 3-6 months
