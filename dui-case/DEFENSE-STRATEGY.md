## 🔴 PILLAR 1: Medical Defense - Contact Lens Irritation

### The Defense
**Claim:** Bloodshot/red eyes were caused by blue contact lens irritation, NOT alcohol intoxication.

### Why This Matters
- Bloodshot eyes are a PRIMARY indicator officers use to establish probable cause for DUI
- Officers almost certainly documented "bloodshot/red/watery eyes" as intoxication indicator
- Medical explanation ELIMINATES this key observation
- Challenges the foundation of officers' probable cause

### Evidence Needed
**Immediate:**
- [ ] Optometrist prescription showing blue contact lenses
- [ ] Date of prescription (proves regular use)
- [ ] Medical records noting eye irritation/dryness
- [ ] Contact lens brand documentation

**For Trial:**
- [ ] Optometrist expert testimony
- [ ] Medical literature: Contact lens irritation symptoms
- [ ] Research showing blue contacts specifically cause irritation
- [ ] Photos/diagrams showing how contacts cause redness

### Legal Argument
```
Officers observed bloodshot/red eyes and attributed this to alcohol intoxication.
However, Mr. Urbay wears blue contact lenses prescribed by his optometrist.
Medical literature confirms contact lens wear, particularly colored lenses,
commonly causes:
  - Eye redness/bloodshot appearance
  - Eye irritation
  - Watery eyes
  - Dry eyes

These symptoms are IDENTICAL to those officers attribute to intoxication.
The medical explanation is documented, provable, and eliminates this
key "intoxication indicator" from the state's case.
```

### Strength Assessment
**🟢 STRONG DEFENSE** if we can document:
1. ✅ Rauli wears blue contacts (easy to prove)
2. ✅ Contacts cause eye irritation (medical fact)
3. ✅ Officers noted bloodshot eyes (likely in report)
4. ✅ Officers attributed to alcohol (standard DUI procedure)

### Action Items
1. **NOW:** Get optometrist records (before attorney meeting)
2. **Soon:** Research medical literature on contact lens irritation
3. **Trial prep:** Arrange optometrist expert witness
4. **Trial prep:** Prepare demonstrative evidence (contact lens samples, medical diagrams)

## 🟠 PILLAR 3: Open Container Possession Defense

### The Defense
**Claim:** 12 empty cans found in vehicle were NOT Rauli's and were not in his possession/control, eliminating open container charges.

### Why This Matters
- Open container laws require POSSESSION, not just presence
- Location of containers is critical (driver's reach vs. back seat vs. trunk)
- Multiple people may have had access to vehicle
- Ownership can be disputed

### Legal Standard
**Arizona open container law requires:**
1. Container must be "in possession" of driver
2. Possession = knowing control over the item
3. Mere presence in vehicle ≠ automatic possession

**Defense:** Containers not in Rauli's possession if:
- Located outside driver's immediate reach
- Belonged to passenger or previous driver
- Rauli lacked knowledge they were there
- Rauli denied ownership at scene

### Evidence Needed
**From Videos:**
- [ ] Marino BWC: Exact location of 12 cans
  - Front seat? Back seat? Trunk? Floor?
  - Driver's reach or inaccessible?
  - Clustered or spread out?
- [ ] Any discussion of ownership on video
- [ ] Rauli's statements about the cans

**From Documents:**
- [ ] Incident report: Container location description
- [ ] Incident report: Ownership claims/statements
- [ ] Vehicle ownership information
- [ ] Passenger information (if any)

**Additional Investigation:**
- [ ] Who else had access to vehicle?
- [ ] When was vehicle last used by someone else?
- [ ] Were there passengers at time of stop?
- [ ] Witnesses who can verify cans weren't Rauli's?

### Possible Scenarios

**Best Case:** Cans in trunk or back area, inaccessible to driver
- **Impact:** Strong possession defense, may eliminate charges
- **Argument:** "Not in driver's possession or control"

**Middle Case:** Cans in back seat, passenger claimed ownership
- **Impact:** Medium defense, challenges direct possession
- **Argument:** "Belonged to passenger, not driver"

**Worst Case:** Cans in front seat/cupholder, within driver's reach
- **Impact:** Weak possession defense
- **Pivot:** Focus on other defense pillars

### Strength Assessment
**🟠 MEDIUM** - Depends on container location and statements
- If outside driver's reach: **Strong defense**
- If ownership disputed on video: **Medium defense**
- If in driver's immediate possession: **Weak defense**
- Unknown until video reviewed

4. **Investigation:** Gather witness statements (if applicable)
