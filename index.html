HCG (Human Chorionic Gonadotropin)
SubQ TRT synergy
S-TIER
Why it's here
Maintains intratesticular testosterone (ITT), preserves fertility, prevents testicular atrophy on TRT. Supports neurosteroid production (pregnenolone, DHEA) that exogenous T suppresses. Non-negotiable on TRT.
Dosing
250 IU SubQ every other day (or 500 IU 2x/week). Total: 750–1000 IU/week.
Timing
Morning injection, any day. Rotate injection sites (abdomen, thigh).
Cycle
Continuous alongside TRT. No cycling needed.
Synergies
Anastrozole (controls HCG-induced E2 spike), TRT base
Side Effects
↑ Estradiol (manage with AI), water retention, rare gyno. Monitor E2 closely.
Source
Peptide Sciences · Prescription via TRT clinic
Anastrozole (Arimidex)
Oral As needed
A-TIER
Why it's here
Aromatase inhibitor — controls estradiol on TRT + HCG. Prevents gyno, bloating, mood crashes from high E2. Use sparingly; crashed E2 is worse than high E2.
Dosing
0.25–0.5 mg 2x/week — adjust based on bloodwork. Target E2: 20–35 pg/mL (sensitive assay).
Timing
On injection days (with TRT or HCG).
Cycle
Continuous with TRT. PRN if E2 stays in range naturally.
Side Effects
Joint pain, fatigue, brain fog if E2 crashes too low. Less is more. Bloodwork is king.
Source
TRT clinic prescription · Amazon (research)

Growth Hormone Axis
CJC-1295/Ipamorelin is the gold standard GHRH+GHRP combo. MK-677 is the oral convenience option but with more side effects. For advanced users, running both synergizes.
CJC-1295 (DAC)
SubQ Stack with Ipa
S-TIER
Why it's here
Long-acting GHRH analog. The DAC (Drug Affinity Complex) extends half-life to ~8 days. Provides sustained, elevated GH baseline — 2-10x GH increase lasting 6+ days. More physiological than exogenous HGH.
Dosing
2 mg SubQ once per week (with DAC). Without DAC (mod GRF 1-29): 100 mcg 2-3x/day.
Timing
Before bed for amplified nocturnal GH pulse. Fast 2 hours prior — insulin blunts GH release.
Cycle
12 weeks on, 4 weeks off. Can run longer at lower doses.
Synergies
Ipamorelin (GHRP amplifier), MK-677 (oral bridge during off-cycle)
Side Effects
Water retention, tingling/numbness in hands (carpal tunnel from elevated GH), increased hunger. Rarely: elevated fasting glucose.
Source

SS-31 (Elamipretide)
SubQ Mitochondria

Dosing
0.5–1 mg SubQ daily (clinical: 0.05 mg/kg/hr IV, but SubQ protocols are 0.5-2mg daily for biohackers).

Morning. Consistent daily dosing for maximum mitochondrial benefit.
Cycle
8–12 weeks on, 4 weeks off. Some protocols run 16 weeks.
Synergies

Injection site reactions. Generally well-tolerated in clinical trials. Long-term data emerging.

Oral 8-week cycle

Dosing
10–20 mg oral daily. Start at 10mg, assess at week 4, increase to 15-20mg if tolerated. On TRT: 10mg is usually sufficient.
Timing
Morning with breakfast. 24-hour half-life, once daily.
Cycle
8 weeks max. Liver values need monitoring at week 4 and end of cycle.
PCT (if not on TRT)
Enclomiphene 12.5–25 mg/day for 4 weeks. On TRT: no PCT needed, TRT is your base.
Liver Support
MANDATORY: NAC 1200mg/day + TUDCA 500mg/day throughout cycle.
Side Effects
⚠️ Liver stress (monitor ALT/AST), mild hair shedding (androgen-mediated), aggression, lipid impact (↓ HDL). Suppressive to natural T (irrelevant on TRT).

Timing
Once weekly SubQ. Same day each week. Abdomen rotation.
Cycle
Run as needed for body composition goals. Slow taper off (don't cold turkey).
Synergies
Ostarine (muscle preservation on cut), TRT (prevents muscle catabolism), MOTS-c
Side Effects
⚠️ GI: nausea, vomiting, diarrhea (dose-dependent, worst during titration). Reduced appetite (feature, not bug). Possible gallstone risk. Rare: pancreatitis. Titrate slowly.

Risks & Legal Disclaimers
⚠️ THIS PROTOCOL CARRIES SIGNIFICANT RISKS:

• SARMs are not FDA-approved for human use. They are sold as "research chemicals." Buying for personal use may violate laws in your jurisdiction. WADA-banned.

• Peptides like BPC-157 and TB-500 have NO completed human clinical trials. You are the experiment. WADA-banned.

• 9-Me-BC has significant phototoxicity risk and unknown long-term safety. Only in-vitro and animal data exists.

• Dihexa activates HGF/MET — a pathway implicated in cancer metastasis. Anyone with cancer history or family risk should NOT use this compound.

• Retatrutide is an investigational drug not yet FDA-approved. Research-grade sources may contain impurities.

• Liver damage is a real risk when stacking oral SARMs, especially RAD-140. Monitor ALT/AST religiously.

• Cardiovascular risk from TRT + SARMs is compounded: LDL rises, HDL crashes, hematocrit elevates. Regular blood donations and lipid monitoring are essential.

• Hormonal disruption — even on TRT, adding SARMs creates an abnormal androgen environment. Side effects include acne, hair loss, mood changes.

• This document is NOT medical advice. It is a research compilation for educational purposes. Consult a licensed physician before using any compound listed here. The author assumes no liability.

• Legal status varies by country. SARMs are illegal to sell for human consumption in the US (legal as research chemicals). Peptides exist in a gray area. Check your local laws.
