#### 1.1 Testosterone Replacement Therapy (TRT)

**What it is:** Exogenous testosterone (typically Testosterone Cypionate or Enanthate) administered via intramuscular or subcutaneous injection to maintain supraphysiological or high-normal testosterone levels.

**Mechanism:** Binds androgen receptors throughout the body — skeletal muscle, bone, brain, and sexual organs. Drives protein synthesis, nitrogen retention, red blood cell production, libido, mood, and cognitive function.

**Why it's included:** Rauli is already on TRT. This is the bedrock of the entire protocol. Without optimized testosterone, everything else underperforms. TRT provides:
- Anabolic environment for muscle growth
- Improved mood, motivation, and cognitive clarity
- Enhanced fat loss (testosterone increases lipolysis)
- Stronger bones (testosterone → osteoblast activity)
- Cardiovascular support (within physiological ranges)

**Dosage:** 100–200mg/week (typical TRT range). Aim for trough levels of 700–1000 ng/dL total T, with free T in the upper quartile of reference range.

**Timing:** Split into 2–3 injections per week for stable blood levels (e.g., Mon/Wed/Fri or Mon/Thu).

**Cycling:** Continuous — TRT is lifelong once natural production is suppressed.

**Administration:** IM (vastus lateralis, deltoid, ventrogluteal) or SubQ (abdominal fat pad). 25–29g needle, 0.5–1" depending on injection site.

**Synergies:** 
- HCG (maintains testicular function, fertility, pregnenolone/DHEA production)
- Boron (increases free testosterone by reducing SHBG)
- Zinc (supports 5-alpha reductase and aromatase metabolism)
- Creatine (amplifies DHT conversion and muscle performance)

**Side effects:** Polycythemia (elevated hematocrit — donate blood if >52%), acne, hair thinning (DHT-sensitive individuals), testicular atrophy (mitigate with HCG), potential cardiovascular risk at supraphysiological levels, estrogen elevation.

**Bloodwork markers:** Total T, Free T, SHBG, Estradiol (sensitive assay), Hematocrit/Hemoglobin, PSA, Lipid panel.

**Tier:** ⭐ S-TIER — The foundation. Everything else is secondary.

#### 1.2 Human Chorionic Gonadotropin (HCG)

**What it is:** A glycoprotein hormone that mimics luteinizing hormone (LH). When exogenous testosterone suppresses LH/FSH via negative feedback, HCG maintains Leydig cell stimulation.

**Mechanism:** Binds LH receptors on Leydig cells → stimulates intratesticular testosterone production → preserves testicular volume and function. Critically, this maintains the entire steroidogenic cascade: cholesterol → pregnenolone → progesterone → DHEA → androstenedione → testosterone.

**Why it's included:** On TRT alone, the testes atrophy and stop producing pregnenolone, DHEA, progesterone, and other neurosteroids. Many men on TRT-only report "feeling flat" despite good testosterone numbers. HCG fills this gap. It also preserves fertility (spermatogenesis) if future children are desired.

**Justification over alternatives:**
- Enclomiphene stimulates LH at the pituitary level but doesn't work well alongside exogenous T
- DHEA/Pregnenolone supplementation (oral) has poor bioavailability and doesn't maintain testicular function
- HCG is the gold standard adjunct to TRT per the AUA guidelines

**Dosage:** 250–500 IU, 2–3x per week (total 500–1500 IU/week). Higher doses (>1500 IU/week) increase estrogen conversion and are rarely needed.

**Timing:** On TRT injection days or alternating days.

**Cycling:** Continuous alongside TRT. Some practitioners cycle 3 months on / 1 month off to prevent desensitization, though evidence for this is debated.

**Administration:** SubQ injection (abdominal fat pad). Reconstitute with bacteriostatic water (5000 IU vial + 5ml BAC water = 1000 IU/ml).

**Synergies:**
- TRT (the obvious partner)
- Pregnenolone supplementation (HCG drives endogenous production, but supplementation can augment)
- DHEA (same reasoning)

**Side effects:** Estrogen elevation (HCG-derived testosterone aromatizes heavily), water retention, nipple sensitivity. May need AI adjustment.

**Best source:** Prescription from TRT clinic (Empower Pharmacy, Hallandale Compounding). Research-grade: Peptide Sciences.

**Tier:** ⭐ S-TIER — Essential TRT adjunct.

#### 1.3 Aromatase Inhibitor (AI) Management

**What it is:** Aromatase inhibitors (Anastrozole, Exemestane) block the enzyme aromatase, which converts testosterone to estradiol (E2).

**Mechanism:** Competitive (Anastrozole) or suicidal (Exemestane) inhibition of aromatase → reduced estrogen → prevents gynecomastia, water retention, and mood disturbances.

**Why it's included:** On TRT + HCG, aromatization can push E2 into problematic ranges (>50 pg/mL sensitive assay). However, **estrogen is NOT the enemy** — it's cardioprotective, neuroprotective, and essential for bone health and libido. The goal is optimization, not elimination.

**Target E2:** 20–35 pg/mL (sensitive LC/MS assay). Some men feel best at 25, others at 40. Individual response varies.

**Dosage:**
- Anastrozole: 0.25–0.5mg, 2x/week (start low)
- Exemestane: 12.5mg, 2x/week (preferred — also has mild androgenic effects and positive lipid impact)

**Cycling:** Continuous alongside TRT. Adjust based on bloodwork, not symptoms alone.

**Administration:** Oral.

**Side effects:** Crashed E2 is WORSE than elevated E2. Symptoms: joint pain, depression, zero libido, dry skin, fatigue. If in doubt, skip a dose.

**Synergies:**
- DIM (diindolylmethane) — promotes favorable 2-OH estrogen metabolism
- Calcium D-Glucarate — supports estrogen detoxification via glucuronidation
- Boron — may modulate SHBG and free E2

**Tier:** 🅰️ A-TIER — Necessary tool, but use with restraint. Many men on TRT do NOT need an AI.

#### 1.4 DHEA (Dehydroepiandrosterone)

**What it is:** A steroid hormone precursor produced primarily by the adrenal glands and (when stimulated by LH/HCG) the testes.

**Mechanism:** DHEA → androstenedione → testosterone/estrone. Also converts to DHEA-S (sulfated form), which is a reservoir. DHEA itself has neurosteroid activity — it modulates GABA-A receptors (excitatory) and sigma-1 receptors (neuroprotective).

**Why it's included:** On TRT, exogenous testosterone suppresses the HPG axis, reducing endogenous DHEA production. Even with HCG, DHEA-S levels often decline. Low DHEA-S correlates with:
- Accelerated aging
- Reduced immune function
- Cognitive decline
- Increased cardiovascular risk

**Dosage:** 25–50mg/day orally (men). Check DHEA-S levels — target 350–500 mcg/dL.

**Timing:** Morning (mimics circadian rhythm).

**Cycling:** Continuous. Monitor with bloodwork.

**Administration:** Oral capsule. Micronized form preferred for better absorption.

**Side effects:** Acne (converts to DHT), oily skin, irritability at high doses. Can elevate estrogen in some men.

**Tier:** 🅰️ A-TIER — Insurance policy for neurosteroid/adrenal health.

#### 1.5 Pregnenolone

**What it is:** The "mother hormone" — first steroid hormone synthesized from cholesterol. Precursor to ALL other steroid hormones (progesterone, DHEA, cortisol, testosterone, estrogen, aldosterone).

**Mechanism:** Cholesterol → Pregnenolone (via CYP11A1 enzyme in mitochondria). From there, it branches into every steroidogenic pathway. Also a potent neurosteroid — enhances memory, modulates GABA-A and NMDA receptors.

**Why it's included:** TRT suppresses endogenous pregnenolone production. Supplementation restores the upstream precursor pool. Many men report improved:
- Cognitive clarity ("the fog lifts")
- Sense of wellbeing
- Stress resilience
- Joint comfort

**Dosage:** 50–100mg/day orally. Start at 50mg. Some men need only 25mg.

**Timing:** Morning.

**Administration:** Oral capsule (micronized). Sublingual may offer better bioavailability.

**Side effects:** Headache, irritability, insomnia (at high doses — it's stimulatory for some). Can convert to progesterone (calming) or DHEA (stimulating) — individual response varies.

**Tier:** 🅰️ A-TIER — Critical neurosteroid support on TRT.

- Sustained focus and motivation

### 9. 💪 SARMs — EVALUATION

---

#### SARMs on TRT: Worth It?

**The question:** Rauli is already on TRT. SARMs (Selective Androgen Receptor Modulators) were designed to provide anabolic effects with tissue selectivity. But on TRT, you already have a full androgen receptor agonist (testosterone) circulating. Do SARMs add anything?

**The verdict: Generally NO for someone on TRT.**

Here's why:

1. **RAD-140 (Testolone):** Most potent SARM. Dry, hard look. BUT: competes with testosterone for androgen receptors. On TRT, you're flooding receptors with real testosterone — adding RAD-140 creates AR competition, not synergy. Also: hepatotoxic (requires TUDCA), suppressive (irrelevant on TRT but indicates systemic stress).

2. **LGD-4033 (Ligandrol):** "Wet" SARM, similar to Dbol. Water retention, good for mass. On TRT, testosterone already provides anabolic signaling. LGD-4033 adds marginal benefit with additional liver stress and lipid disruption.

3. **MK-2866 (Ostarine):** Mildest SARM. Good for cuts. On TRT, testosterone already preserves muscle during caloric deficit (especially with Retatrutide). Ostarine is redundant.

4. **S-23:** Very potent, very suppressive, near-steroid profile. If you want something this strong, just increase TRT dose or add a real anabolic (Anavar, Primobolan) under medical supervision.

5. **YK-11:** Technically a myostatin inhibitor/follistatin stimulator, not a true SARM. Interesting mechanism but extremely limited data, hepatotoxic, and unknown long-term risks.

**Exception:** If Rauli wanted to add an oral anabolic for a blast phase, pharmaceutical-grade Anavar (Oxandrolone) 20–50mg/day for 6–8 weeks would be vastly superior to any SARM — better studied, predictable, effective, and available by prescription. Pair with TUDCA + NAC for liver support.

**Final ruling:** SARMs are a "second-best" solution designed for people who can't or won't use testosterone. On TRT, they're redundant or inferior to real anabolics.

**Tier:** ❌ ALL EXCLUDED — Redundant on TRT. Use pharmaceutical anabolics if blast is desired.

### 13. 🧪 IMMUNE & LONGEVITY

#### 13.1 Rapamycin (Sirolimus) — Microdose Protocol

**What it is:** An mTOR inhibitor originally developed as an immunosuppressant for organ transplant recipients. At LOW doses, it paradoxically enhances immune function and extends lifespan.

**Mechanism:**
- Inhibits mTORC1 → activates autophagy (cellular cleanup)
- At low doses: primarily inhibits mTORC1 (growth, aging) while sparing mTORC2 (immune function, glucose metabolism)
- Extends lifespan in every organism tested (yeast, worms, flies, mice)
- The PEARL trial (first human longevity trial) shows slowed muscle and bone aging
- Reduces senescent cell accumulation
- Enhances vaccine response in elderly

**Why it's included:** Rapamycin is the single most robust lifespan-extending intervention known. Peter Attia, David Sinclair, and other longevity physicians take it themselves. For Rauli:
- Autophagy activation (cellular cleanup — complements senolytics)
- mTOR modulation (balances growth vs. longevity signaling)
- Immune enhancement (paradoxical at low doses)
- Potential cancer prevention (mTOR is overactive in most cancers)

**CRITICAL NUANCE:** mTOR activation is NEEDED for muscle growth. Rapamycin must be cycled to avoid chronic mTOR suppression, which would impair hypertrophy. Take on rest days or during deload weeks.

**Dosage:** 5–6mg once weekly (Peter Diamandis protocol). Start at 3mg weekly and titrate.

**Timing:** One day per week, typically on a rest day. Some take it every other week.

**Cycling:** Weekly dosing is inherently cycled (drug clears by next dose). Some do 6 weeks on / 2 weeks off. Avoid during intense training blocks where muscle growth is the priority.

**Administration:** Oral (prescription required — sirolimus tablets).

**Side effects:** Mouth sores/ulcers (most common at longevity doses), impaired wound healing, potential immunosuppression (at high/chronic doses), lipid elevation, glucose impairment. Monitor closely.

**Best source:** Prescription only. Compounding pharmacies (Empower, Hallandale). Some longevity clinics prescribe off-label.

**Tier:** 🅰️ A-TIER — Most robust longevity compound known, but requires medical supervision and careful cycling around training.

#### 13.2 NMN (Nicotinamide Mononucleotide) — NEW ADDITION

**What it is:** A direct precursor to NAD+ (nicotinamide adenine dinucleotide), the essential coenzyme for cellular energy production, DNA repair, and sirtuin activation.

**Mechanism:**
- NMN → NAD+ (via NMNAT enzymes)
- NAD+ activates sirtuins (SIRT1–7) — longevity genes
- NAD+ is essential for PARP (DNA repair enzyme)
- NAD+ levels decline ~50% between ages 40–60
- Restoring NAD+ has been shown to reverse multiple aging phenotypes in mice

**Why NMN over NR (Nicotinamide Riboside):**
- Recent evidence suggests NMN is more bioavailable than NR
- NMN doesn't require the rate-limiting NRK enzyme step
- David Sinclair (Harvard longevity researcher) personally uses NMN
- Both are effective — NMN has slightly more momentum in the field

**Dosage:** 500–1000mg/day. Start at 500mg.

**Synergies:**

- Rapamycin (complementary longevity pathways — mTOR inhibition + sirtuin activation)

**Side effects:** Very well tolerated. Possible: flushing (NAD+ can activate niacin pathways), GI upset at high doses.

**Best source:** ProHealth Longevity, DoNotAge, Renue By Science (liposomal).

**Tier:** ⭐ S-TIER — The NAD+ restorer.

7. Rotate injection sites to prevent lipodystrophy
